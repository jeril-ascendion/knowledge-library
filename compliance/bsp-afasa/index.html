BSP / AFASA (Philippines)

Architecture and compliance obligations for BSP-supervised financial institutions in the Philippines

TOGAF ADM NIST CSF ISO 27001 AWS Well-Arch Google SRE AI-Native
💡
In Plain English

The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) is the central bank that regulates all banks and financial institutions in the Philippines. They have rules for how technology systems must be built and operated. AFASA is the framework that defines what architecture standards these institutions must follow.

📈
Business Value

Non-compliance with BSP regulations can result in monetary penalties, operational restrictions, and reputational damage. More importantly, BSP requirements encode hard-won lessons about building resilient financial systems that protect Filipino depositors and financial market stability.

📖 Detailed Explanation

The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas Technology Supervision Group oversees the technology risk management practices of approximately 700 banks and quasi-banks, plus hundreds of payment system operators and money service businesses. Their regulatory framework has evolved significantly since 2020, driven by digital transformation, increased cybersecurity threats, and the pandemic-accelerated shift to digital financial services.

BSP Circular 982 — Technology Risk Management (TRM) is the primary technology regulation for BSP-supervised financial institutions (BSFIs). It establishes a comprehensive framework covering: Information Security (IS) governance, IS risk management, IS controls, third-party risk management, technology operations, and business continuity management. Key architectural implications: every BSFI must maintain a Technology Risk Register, designate a Technology Risk Officer, maintain documented recovery time objectives (RTO ≤ 4 hours) and recovery point objectives (RPO ≤ 2 hours) for Tier 1 systems, and conduct annual business continuity drills with documented results.

BSP Circular 1169 — Cybersecurity Incident Reporting mandates that BSFIs report significant cybersecurity incidents to the BSP within specific timeframes: incidents affecting critical systems must be reported within 2 hours of discovery; incidents affecting customer data must be reported within 10 days with a comprehensive incident report. Architecture implication: every BSFI must deploy a Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system capable of detecting and classifying cybersecurity events in real time, and must maintain an incident response workflow that can generate BSP-format reports.

AFASA — Advance Framework for Architecture Standards and Accountability (internal BSP guidance) establishes expectations for architecture governance at BSFIs. While not a standalone circular, AFASA principles are referenced in BSP examination findings. Key requirements: formal Architecture Review Board with documented composition and authority; Architecture Decision Records for all significant technology decisions; published technology roadmaps showing planned modernization and risk reduction; system architecture documentation at the component level (C4 equivalent) for all Tier 1 systems.

BSP Digital Banking Framework (Circular 1105 and subsequent guidance) sets additional requirements for digital banks and digital financial services: API security standards aligned with PCI DSS; strong customer authentication (SCA) for digital transactions; transaction monitoring for fraud detection with defined false positive thresholds; data residency — customer financial data for Philippine residents must remain within Philippine jurisdiction unless explicit BSP approval is obtained for offshore storage.

BSP Technology Examination Process: The BSP's Technology Supervision Group (TSG) conducts on-site and off-site technology examinations, typically annually for universal and commercial banks, less frequently for rural banks and NBFIs. Examination scope: IS governance documentation, technology risk register completeness, BC/DR drill evidence, cybersecurity control effectiveness, third-party vendor risk management evidence, and architectural documentation of critical systems. Findings are rated by severity: Matter Requiring Attention (MRA), Minor Finding, Major Finding, or Critical Finding. Major and Critical Findings trigger remediation timelines and follow-up examinations.

The Data Residency Challenge: Philippine banks expanding into digital channels face the challenge of cloud adoption while meeting BSP data residency requirements. AWS, Azure, and GCP all have local regions in the Philippines (Philippines/ap-southeast-1 region strategy). Architecture solutions: deploy core banking and customer PII workloads in Philippine-region cloud deployments; non-PII workloads (analytics, DevOps tooling, CDN) can use international regions; apply AWS data perimeter controls, Azure Policy, or GCP organization policies to enforce residency boundaries technically, providing auditable evidence to the BSP.

🌟 Core Principles

1
Compliance as Architecture Input, Not Output

BSP requirements are architecture constraints to be modeled in the design phase — not audit findings to be remediated after deployment. RTO/RPO, data residency, and audit trail requirements are first-class NFRs.

2
Evidence-First Architecture

Every architecture decision with compliance implications must produce auditable evidence: documented DR test results, SIEM alert logs, access control configurations, and architecture diagrams. BSP examiners evaluate documentation quality, not intentions.

3
Defense in Depth Satisfies Multiple Circulars

A layered security architecture (network segmentation + application-level controls + monitoring + incident response) simultaneously satisfies requirements across Circular 982 (security controls), Circular 1169 (incident detection), and PCI DSS (for card-issuing banks). Design once, satisfy many.

4
Data Residency is Non-Negotiable for PII

Customer financial data (account balances, transaction history, KYC documents) for Philippine residents must remain within Philippine jurisdiction unless BSP grants explicit written approval. Design the data classification and residency architecture before selecting cloud providers.

⚙️ Implementation Steps

1

Map Applicable BSP Circulars to Architecture Requirements

Create a compliance matrix: list each applicable circular, extract the specific architecture requirements from each, and map to the architectural component responsible for satisfying it. This becomes your compliance design specification.

2

Establish the Technology Risk Register

Document every technology risk (cyber threats, vendor concentration, technology obsolescence, single points of failure) with: risk description, likelihood (1-5), impact (1-5), risk score, control owner, control effectiveness, residual risk, and review date. Review quarterly with the Technology Risk Officer.

3

Design the BC/DR Architecture Against RTO/RPO

For each Tier 1 system, design the recovery architecture to meet RTO ≤ 4 hours and RPO ≤ 2 hours. Document the specific recovery procedure (runbook), the failover trigger (manual vs. automated), and the data replication mode (synchronous vs. asynchronous) that enables the RPO.

4

Deploy SIEM with BSP Circular 1169 Playbooks

Implement SIEM with detection rules aligned to BSP Circular 1169 reportable incident categories: unauthorized access, data exfiltration, DDoS, ransomware. For each rule: detection logic, alert severity, response playbook, and escalation path to the Technology Risk Officer for 2-hour reporting compliance.

5

Prepare the BSP Examination Evidence Repository

Maintain a continuously updated documentation repository accessible to BSP examiners: architecture diagrams (updated quarterly), technology risk register (updated monthly), DR drill reports (annual), penetration test reports (annual), policy and procedure documents, and access control configurations.

◈ Recommended Patterns

✦ Compliance-as-Code

BSP architectural requirements expressed as automated tests and policy rules (OPA, AWS Config Rules, Azure Policy). Drift from compliant configuration triggers automated alerts. Evidence is generated automatically by CI/CD pipelines, not assembled manually before examinations.

✦ Dual-Site Synchronous Replication

Primary data center in Metro Manila + DR site in Cebu or Clark, connected via dedicated MPLS or fiber. Synchronous database replication achieves RPO ≈ 0 for core banking. Annual failover drill with documented RTO measurement. The standard HA/DR architecture for Tier 1 BSP-regulated systems.

✦ Regulatory Reporting Pipeline

Automated data pipeline that generates BSP-mandated reports (CITEM reports, CAR, SES, DOSRI, ROE) from core banking data sources on the required regulatory schedule, with data lineage documentation satisfying BSP audit requirements.

⛔ Anti-Patterns to Avoid

⛔ Manual BSP Compliance Reports

Generating BSP regulatory reports manually from spreadsheets assembled by querying multiple systems. Prone to errors, inconsistencies, and delayed submission. BSP examiners increasingly expect automated, auditable data pipelines for supervisory reporting.

⛔ Data Residency by Trust

Assuming that cloud providers will honor data residency commitments without technical enforcement. Apply AWS Service Control Policies, Azure Policy, or GCP Organization Policies to technically prevent data from being stored outside approved regions — auditable evidence of residency enforcement.

⛔ BC/DR Plan Without Test Results

Maintaining a BC/DR plan document that has never been executed. BSP examination finding #1: 'The institution's BC/DR plan has not been tested within the required annual timeframe.' Untested plans are a Critical Finding.

🤖 AI Augmentation Extensions

🤖 AI-Assisted Regulatory Monitoring

Agents monitor BSP issuance announcements and automatically map new circulars to the existing compliance matrix, identifying gaps that require architecture changes and generating impact assessment drafts for the Technology Risk Officer's review.

⚡ New BSP requirements must be reviewed by a qualified compliance officer and legal counsel before being added to the architecture implementation backlog. AI mapping is a starting point, not a final determination.
🤖 Automated Evidence Package Generation

Before each BSP examination, AI agents compile the evidence repository: pull the latest architecture diagrams from the documentation system, extract the most recent technology risk register, aggregate DR drill reports, and generate an examination-ready package with a table of contents aligned to the examination scope.

⚡ Evidence packages require review and sign-off by the Technology Risk Officer before submission to BSP examiners.
